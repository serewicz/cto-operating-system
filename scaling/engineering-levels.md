# Engineering Levels Framework

## Purpose

Define a simple engineering levels framework that clarifies scope, ownership, expectations, and readiness signals.

## When to Use It

- Creating or improving engineering career paths
- Calibrating hiring, promotion, and performance expectations
- Scaling from informal roles to repeatable management practices
- Clarifying expectations across individual contributor and management tracks

## Key Questions

- What scope should each level own?
- What behavior signals readiness for the next level?
- Where is the organization asking people to operate beyond their current support structure?
- How should leadership distinguish technical skill from ownership maturity?

## Template or Framework

| Level | Scope | Ownership | Expectations | Signals of Readiness |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Junior Engineer | Tasks within a defined area | Owns assigned work with guidance | Learns codebase, asks good questions, writes maintainable code, responds to feedback | Completes scoped work reliably and understands local impact |
| Engineer | Features or components | Owns implementation through delivery | Makes reasonable tradeoffs, tests work, communicates blockers, improves system understanding | Delivers independently and anticipates dependencies |
| Senior Engineer | Complex features, services, or cross-team work | Owns technical approach and delivery risk | Designs solutions, mentors others, handles ambiguity, improves quality and reliability | Leads important work without needing constant direction |
| Staff/Lead Engineer | Multi-team technical direction or critical systems | Owns architecture, standards, and cross-team alignment | Sets technical direction, resolves tradeoffs, reduces systemic risk, raises engineering quality | Influences beyond team and improves organizational execution |
| Engineering Manager | Team outcomes and people leadership | Owns delivery, coaching, hiring, performance, and team health | Creates clarity, manages capacity, develops people, escalates risks, aligns with product | Team delivers predictably and people grow under management |
| Director | Multiple teams or a major function | Owns organizational capability and execution system | Builds leaders, manages portfolio priorities, coordinates across functions, connects strategy to execution | Function scales without relying on heroic individual effort |

## Expected Outcome

A practical levels framework that improves hiring, promotion, coaching, and role clarity without overengineering career architecture.
