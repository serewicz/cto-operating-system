# Engineering Hiring Framework

## Purpose

Create a hiring framework that connects role definition, business need, technical scope, evaluation process, and first-month expectations.

## When to Use It

- Opening a new engineering, platform, security, data, AI, or leadership role
- Deciding between full-time hiring, vendor support, fractional leadership, or buying a solution
- Improving interview consistency and reducing unclear hiring signals
- Scaling engineering after founder-led or informal hiring

## Key Questions

- What business need requires this role?
- What technical scope and ownership must the person carry?
- What does success look like in the first 30 days?
- What interview evidence will distinguish good candidates from plausible candidates?
- Should the company build, buy, hire, or use fractional support?

### Business Need

Explain why this role matters now. Tie it to growth, delivery, platform reliability, security, AI governance, cost, customer commitments, or leadership capacity.

### Technical Scope

Define systems, domains, platforms, processes, teams, or decisions the role will own.

### Interview Process

| Stage | Purpose | Signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Recruiter or hiring screen | Confirm motivation, communication, compensation, and basic fit | Clear interest and role alignment |
| Hiring manager interview | Evaluate scope, ownership, judgment, and operating style | Evidence of relevant outcomes |
| Technical or leadership exercise | Test practical decision-making | Clear tradeoffs and structured thinking |
| Cross-functional interview | Evaluate product, security, finance, or executive collaboration | Ability to align outside engineering |
| Final review | Compare evidence against scorecard | Hiring decision tied to role need |

### Red Flags

- Cannot explain tradeoffs
- Optimizes for technology preference without business context
- Lacks ownership of outcomes
- Avoids accountability for production, quality, or risk
- Communicates in vague or overly technical language for executive audiences
- Has no practical view of security, cost, or operations

### First 30-Day Expectations

- Understand business priorities and team context
- Build relationships with key stakeholders
- Review relevant systems, processes, and risks
- Own a small but meaningful improvement
- Produce a clear 30-day readout

### Build vs Buy vs Fractional Decision

Use hiring only when the need is durable, strategic, and requires internal ownership. Use vendors or products when the capability is commodity or time-sensitive. Use fractional leadership when the company needs executive-level judgment before it is ready for a full-time hire.

## Expected Outcome

A hiring process that connects roles to business needs, improves signal quality, and reduces expensive mismatches.
