# Board Technology Report Template

## Purpose

Provide a concise board-ready format for communicating technology posture, priorities, risks, decisions, and next steps.

## When to Use It

- Monthly or quarterly board updates
- Growth equity or investor reporting
- Pre-fundraise or diligence preparation
- Executive review of technology initiatives and risk

## Key Questions

- What should the board know about technology posture?
- Which initiatives are strategically important?
- What risks require board awareness or decision support?
- What changed since the last report?
- What decisions are needed from leadership?

## Template or Framework

### Executive Summary

Briefly summarize technology posture, momentum, material risks, and decisions needed.

### Current Technology Posture

Describe platform health, architecture, infrastructure, security, delivery, team, and cost posture.

### Strategic Initiatives

| Initiative | Business Objective | Status | Owner | Board Relevance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  |  | On Track / At Risk / Blocked |  |  |

### Delivery Status

Summarize roadmap progress, delivery risks, dependencies, and major releases.

### AI Initiatives

Summarize AI use cases, governance status, model/provider choices, risks, costs, and measured outcomes.

### Security Posture

Summarize controls, incidents, access reviews, vulnerabilities, compliance requirements, and remediation progress.

### Risks

| Risk | Business Impact | Owner | Status | Decision Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |  |  |

### Decisions Needed

List decisions the board or executive team must make.

### Metrics

Use metrics that are already tracked and decision-relevant. Avoid inventing metrics solely for reporting.

### Next 30/60/90 Days

Summarize the most important actions and expected outcomes.

## Expected Outcome

A board report that communicates technology in terms of risk, strategy, execution, and decisions rather than raw engineering activity.
