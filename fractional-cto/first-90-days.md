# Fractional CTO First 90 Days

## Purpose

Provide a focused operating plan for a fractional CTO engagement that creates clarity, reduces risk, and establishes executive-level technology cadence quickly.

## When to Use It

- A founder or CEO needs senior technology leadership without a full-time CTO
- A company needs help preparing for funding, diligence, or scale
- Delivery, architecture, security, AI, or hiring decisions need executive structure
- Technology leadership is fragmented or overloaded

## Key Questions

- What business outcomes require technology leadership now?
- Which risks require immediate stabilization?
- What should the CEO, founder, board, or investors see each month?
- Which decisions need a fractional CTO versus a hire, vendor, or internal owner?

## Template or Framework

### Days 1-30: Discovery

- Interview CEO, founders, product, engineering, finance, security, and key customers where appropriate.
- Review architecture, delivery process, infrastructure, security, AI use cases, roadmap, budget, and vendor commitments.
- Identify urgent operational, security, or delivery risks.
- Establish executive reporting cadence.

### Days 31-60: Stabilization and Prioritization

- Create a risk register and technology priority list.
- Clarify ownership for critical systems and decisions.
- Recommend immediate security, infrastructure, or delivery improvements.
- Align roadmap priorities with business objectives.
- Define what should be hired, outsourced, paused, or handled fractionally.

### Days 61-90: Governance and Roadmap

- Establish architecture decision process.
- Create operating cadence for delivery, risk, budget, and AI governance.
- Deliver hiring and vendor recommendations.
- Present 90-day findings and next-quarter roadmap to leadership.

## Expected Outcome

The company should have a clear technology operating model, executive reporting cadence, risk visibility, and a practical roadmap for the next quarter.
