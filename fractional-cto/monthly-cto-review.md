# Monthly CTO Review

## Purpose

Create a monthly operating review for technology leadership that connects progress, risks, decisions, delivery, infrastructure, security, AI, team, budget, and priorities.

## When to Use It

- Monthly CEO or founder review
- Fractional CTO engagement cadence
- Executive operating meeting
- Board packet preparation

## Key Questions

- What progress was made this month?
- What risks require executive attention?
- What decisions are blocked?
- How are delivery, infrastructure, security, AI, team, and budget trending?
- What matters most next month?

## Template or Framework

### Progress

Summarize completed work, meaningful milestones, and outcomes.

### Risks

| Risk | Impact | Owner | Status | Escalation Needed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |  |  |

### Decisions

List decisions needed from CEO, founder, board, product, security, or finance.

### Delivery

Summarize roadmap progress, blocked work, dependencies, quality concerns, and release confidence.

### Infrastructure

Summarize reliability, cloud, Kubernetes, observability, cost, incidents, and operational ownership.

### Security

Summarize access, vulnerabilities, incidents, compliance work, and control improvements.

### AI

Summarize AI use cases, governance status, provider/model choices, risk, cost, and measured value.

### Team

Summarize staffing, leadership capacity, hiring needs, vendor needs, and key-person risk.

### Budget

Summarize spend trends, vendor decisions, cloud cost drivers, and investment needs.

### Priorities for Next Month

List the few priorities that matter most.

## Expected Outcome

A repeatable monthly review that keeps technology leadership tied to business outcomes, risk visibility, and executive decision-making.
