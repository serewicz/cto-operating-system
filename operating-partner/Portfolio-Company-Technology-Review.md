# Portfolio Company Technology Review

## Purpose

Provide a quarterly operating partner review template for evaluating technology posture, progress, risk, and decisions across a portfolio company.

## When to Use

- Quarterly portfolio operating reviews
- Board preparation
- Post-close follow-up
- Annual planning or budget review
- Technology risk escalation

## Quarterly Review Template

### Executive Summary

Summarize technology posture, progress since last review, material risks, decisions needed, and next-quarter priorities.

### Engineering Health

Assess:

- Leadership capacity
- Team structure and role clarity
- Hiring gaps
- Key-person dependency
- Delivery ownership
- Product and engineering alignment
- Technical debt trends

### Security Posture

Assess:

- Identity and privileged access
- Vulnerability management
- Incident response
- Backup and recovery validation
- Logging and monitoring
- Compliance or customer security requirements
- Security roadmap progress

### Cloud Cost

Assess:

- Cloud spend trend
- Kubernetes and infrastructure cost drivers
- Unit economics where available
- Vendor overlap
- Ownership between Engineering and Finance
- Forecasting and review cadence

### Delivery Predictability

Assess:

- Roadmap confidence
- Release quality
- Dependency management
- Planning discipline
- Escalation of blocked work
- Customer-impacting delivery risk

### Board Reporting Format

| Section | Content |
| --- | --- |
| Summary | Technology posture and changes since last review |
| Top Risks | Red and material yellow risks with owners |
| Progress | Completed actions and evidence |
| Decisions Needed | Budget, hiring, vendor, roadmap, security, or AI decisions |
| Next Quarter | Priority actions and expected outcomes |

## Management Questions

- What changed materially since the last review?
- Which technology risks are improving, worsening, or unresolved?
- Which owners are accountable for each material risk?
- What business outcomes depend on technology execution next quarter?
- Which decisions require CEO, board, investor, or operating partner involvement?

## Expected Outcome

A repeatable quarterly review that keeps technology execution, risk, security, cloud cost, delivery, and board communication connected to value creation.
