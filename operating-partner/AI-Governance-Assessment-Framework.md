# AI Governance Assessment Framework

## Purpose

Assess whether a company can use AI responsibly, securely, cost-effectively, and in alignment with business outcomes.

## When to Use

- Before approving AI initiatives or customer-facing AI features
- During technology diligence or portfolio company review
- When leadership needs visibility into AI risk, model choice, and data handling
- When internal teams are using AI tools without a formal governance model

## AI Use Case Inventory

## Data Readiness

Assess:

- Data classification and sensitivity
- Data quality and lineage
- Access control and least privilege
- Retention and deletion requirements
- Customer, regulated, or confidential data exposure
- Readiness for retrieval, search, analytics, or model context

## Model Governance

Review:

- Model/provider selection rationale
- Local vs cloud LLM or SLM tradeoffs
- Model version and evaluation criteria
- Prompt and context management
- Cost and usage monitoring
- Hallucination, bias, and quality controls
- Fallback and escalation behavior

## Human Oversight

Define where human review is required:

- Customer-facing responses
- Financial, legal, compliance, or HR-related output
- Security-sensitive recommendations
- High-cost or irreversible actions
- Board or investor-facing analysis

## Security and Privacy

Assess:

- Prompt injection exposure
- Data leakage risk
- Provider data retention and training policies
- Secrets and credential handling
- Logging of sensitive prompts or outputs
- Access control and audit trails
- Privacy obligations and customer commitments

## Vendor Risk

Review:

- Contract terms
- Data processing and retention
- SOC reports or security attestations
- Provider concentration risk
- Exit strategy and portability
- Cost predictability
- Service availability and support

## Board-Level AI Questions

- Which AI use cases are active, proposed, or already in production?
- What data is exposed to AI systems, and how is it classified?
- Which providers and models are being used, and why?
- What human review exists for high-impact outputs?
- How are AI cost, quality, security, and business value measured?
- What AI use cases are explicitly prohibited or require executive approval?
- What evidence demonstrates that AI governance is operating, not just documented?

## Expected Outcome

An AI governance assessment that makes AI usage, data exposure, model choice, human oversight, vendor risk, cost, and board-level accountability visible.
