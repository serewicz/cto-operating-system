# Technology Due Diligence Checklist

## Purpose

Provide investors, acquirers, and executives with a structured checklist for evaluating technology risk, scalability, and post-close priorities.

## When to Use It

- Growth equity or private equity diligence
- Acquisition diligence and integration planning
- Board-level technology review
- Pre-fundraise technology readiness assessment

## Key Questions

- Can the platform support the investment thesis?
- Which technology risks could affect valuation, integration, growth, or customer retention?
- Are security and data practices appropriate for the company's obligations?
- Where are key-person dependencies or undocumented operational risks?
- What should be addressed in the first 100 days post-close?

## Template or Framework

| Area | Diligence Questions | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Architecture | Is the system understandable, scalable, resilient, and appropriately modular? | Diagrams, service inventory, code review, incident history |
| Infrastructure | How are cloud, Kubernetes, environments, backups, monitoring, and access managed? | Cloud accounts, cluster config, runbooks, dashboards |
| Security | Are identity, secrets, vulnerability management, logging, and incident response mature? | Policies, tools, audit logs, security tickets |
| Data | Is data ownership, lineage, quality, privacy, and retention understood? | Data maps, schemas, pipelines, policies |
| AI | Are AI use cases, models, data sensitivity, human review, and costs governed? | AI inventory, provider settings, prompts, evaluation results |
| Engineering Team | Does the team have enough leadership, seniority, and coverage? | Org chart, interviews, role descriptions |
| Delivery Process | Can the team plan, ship, test, and release predictably? | Roadmap, CI/CD, release history, sprint data |
| Vendor Dependency | Are critical vendors understood, contracted, and replaceable? | Vendor list, contracts, architecture dependencies |
| Open Source Risk | Are licenses, dependencies, vulnerabilities, and maintenance risks managed? | SBOM, dependency scans, license review |
| Scalability | What limits growth in users, data, integrations, or operations? | Load tests, architecture review, cost trends |
| Technical Debt | What slows change or increases operational risk? | Backlog, code review, defect history |
| Key-Person Risk | Which systems, deployments, or decisions depend on one person? | Interviews, access review, documentation |
| Post-Close Priorities | What must happen in the first 30, 60, and 100 days? | Risk register, roadmap, management plan |

## Expected Outcome

A diligence view that identifies material risk, validates the technology story, informs valuation or integration planning, and creates a practical post-close operating agenda.
