# Architecture Decision Record Template

## Purpose

Capture important architecture decisions in a concise format that preserves context, tradeoffs, ownership, and review timing.

## When to Use It

- Selecting major technologies, platforms, vendors, or architecture patterns
- Making decisions with security, cost, operational, or reversibility implications
- Resolving recurring architecture debates
- Creating diligence-ready evidence of technical governance

## Key Questions

- What decision is being made?
- What business or technical context matters?
- Which options were considered?
- What tradeoffs are being accepted?
- When should this decision be reviewed?

## Template or Framework

## ADR Title

Short decision name.

### Status

Proposed / Accepted / Deprecated / Superseded

### Context

Describe the business need, technical constraints, operational context, and decision drivers.

### Decision

State the chosen direction clearly.

### Options Considered

| Option | Summary | Why Accepted or Rejected |
| --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |

### Tradeoffs

List what the organization gains and what it accepts in exchange.

### Security Impact

Describe access, data protection, vulnerability, compliance, and audit implications.

### Cost Impact

Describe expected engineering, vendor, infrastructure, migration, and operational costs.

### Operational Impact

Describe support model, monitoring, on-call, deployment, incident, and skill requirements.

### Reversibility

Describe whether the decision is reversible, how difficult reversal would be, and what would trigger reconsideration.

### Review Date

Set a date or milestone for review.

## Expected Outcome

A decision record that gives future leaders enough context to understand why a technology choice was made and when it should be revisited.
