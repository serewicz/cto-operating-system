# Technology Risk Register Template

## Purpose

Track material technology risks in a format executives can review, own, prioritize, and act on.

## When to Use It

- After a technology assessment, diligence review, incident, audit, or board discussion
- When risks span architecture, security, people, delivery, vendors, infrastructure, or AI
- When leadership needs visibility into risk ownership and mitigation progress

## Key Questions

- What is the risk and why does it matter to the business?
- Who owns mitigation?
- What is the likelihood and severity?
- What is the plan, timeline, and escalation trigger?
- What decision or investment is required?

## Template or Framework

| Risk | Business Impact | Likelihood | Severity | Owner | Mitigation | Timeline | Status | Escalation Trigger |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  | Revenue, customer trust, compliance, operational continuity, valuation, delivery speed | Low / Medium / High | Low / Medium / High / Critical | Named accountable owner | Specific action plan | Date or milestone | Open / In Progress / Mitigated / Accepted | Condition requiring executive review |

### Risk Categories

- Cybersecurity
- Availability and resilience
- Architecture and scalability
- Data and AI governance
- Technical debt
- Vendor concentration
- Key-person dependency
- Delivery predictability
- Infrastructure cost
- Compliance or audit readiness

## Expected Outcome

A living technology risk register that makes ownership, mitigation, timeline, and escalation explicit for management and board review.
