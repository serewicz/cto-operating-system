# Engineering Organization Review

## Purpose

Evaluate whether the engineering organization has the structure, leadership, skills, cadence, and communication patterns needed to deliver business outcomes reliably.

## When to Use It

- During CTO transition, diligence, or post-investment planning
- When delivery is unpredictable or engineering morale is unclear
- When scaling from founder-led engineering to a leadership team
- When deciding whether to hire, reorganize, outsource, or add fractional leadership

## Key Questions

- Does every critical system and decision area have a clear owner?
- Are managers and technical leads operating at the right level?
- Is delivery predictable enough for executive planning?
- Where are hiring gaps or role ambiguity slowing the business?
- Are product, engineering, security, and operations aligned?

## Template or Framework

| Review Area | What to Inspect | Signals of Strength | Warning Signs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Team Structure | Team boundaries, reporting lines, product alignment | Clear ownership and manageable scope | Too many shared queues or unclear accountability |
| Leadership Capacity | Managers, tech leads, staff engineers, directors | Leaders make decisions and coach others | CTO or founder remains bottleneck for routine decisions |
| Delivery Predictability | Planning, estimation, commitments, release cadence | Teams can explain capacity and tradeoffs | Frequent surprise slips or hidden dependencies |
| Hiring Gaps | Role coverage, seniority mix, critical skills | Hiring plan tied to business needs | Hiring based on generic headcount targets |
| Key-Person Risk | Single owners, undocumented systems, access concentration | Shadowing and documentation exist | One person can block release, support, or architecture decisions |
| Communication Patterns | Operating reviews, escalation paths, cross-functional forums | Risks surface early and clearly | Executives hear about problems after commitments miss |
| Product/Engineering Alignment | Roadmap process, discovery, prioritization | Work maps to customer and business outcomes | Engineering becomes feature factory or isolated platform team |
| Metrics and Cadence | Delivery, quality, reliability, cost, security metrics | Metrics inform decisions | Metrics are absent, vanity-based, or punitive |

### Interview Prompts

- What decisions still require the CTO, founder, or VP Engineering?
- Which teams have more work than they can realistically deliver?
- What are the most common causes of delivery delay?
- Which systems are hardest to change safely?
- Where does product believe engineering is underperforming, and where does engineering disagree?

## Expected Outcome

An executive-ready organization review that clarifies leadership gaps, operating cadence, hiring needs, key-person risk, and the changes required to scale engineering responsibly.
