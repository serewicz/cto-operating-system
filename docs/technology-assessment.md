# Technology Assessment

## Purpose

Assess the current state of a company's technology environment and translate technical conditions into business risks, opportunities, and operating priorities.

## When to Use It

- Before a major funding, acquisition, or strategic planning cycle
- When delivery predictability, platform reliability, or security posture is unclear
- When a CEO or board needs a business-level view of technology health
- When a CTO needs to prioritize modernization work

## Key Questions

- Can the current architecture support the business strategy?
- Which technology risks could affect revenue, customer trust, compliance, or operational continuity?
- Where is technical debt slowing delivery or increasing risk?
- Are infrastructure and security practices appropriate for the company's maturity and obligations?
- Are technology investments aligned with measurable business outcomes?

## Template or Framework

| Domain | Assessment Focus | Evidence to Review | Business Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Architecture | System design, boundaries, coupling, scalability, resilience | Diagrams, code structure, service inventory, incident history | Determines whether the platform can support growth and change |
| Infrastructure | Cloud, Kubernetes, networking, environments, observability, backups | Cloud accounts, cluster config, runbooks, monitoring dashboards | Shows operational maturity and reliability risk |
| Security | Identity, access, vulnerabilities, secrets, logging, incident response | IAM, security tools, policies, audit trails, tickets | Connects control gaps to customer, compliance, and reputation risk |
| Delivery Process | Planning, release process, testing, CI/CD, deployment ownership | Roadmaps, sprint data, release notes, deployment logs | Indicates whether commitments are predictable |
| Team Structure | Roles, ownership, leadership coverage, on-call model | Org chart, role descriptions, stakeholder interviews | Reveals capacity, key-person risk, and accountability gaps |
| Vendor Dependencies | Critical vendors, contracts, renewal exposure, switching cost | Vendor list, contracts, invoices, integration maps | Identifies concentration risk and budget exposure |
| Data and AI Readiness | Data quality, governance, privacy, AI use cases, model selection | Data catalog, pipelines, AI pilots, governance docs | Determines whether AI initiatives can be pursued responsibly |
| Technical Debt | Legacy systems, brittle integrations, manual processes, obsolete components | Backlog, architecture review, defect history | Connects engineering drag to speed, cost, and risk |
| Operational Risk | Incidents, support burden, disaster recovery, alerting, runbooks | Incident reports, support tickets, DR tests | Shows exposure to downtime and fragile operations |
| Business Alignment | Strategy, roadmap, customer commitments, investment priorities | OKRs, board materials, roadmap, budget | Tests whether technology work supports business outcomes |

### Rating Guidance

Use a simple red/yellow/green view:

- Red: material risk requiring executive visibility or urgent remediation
- Yellow: meaningful gap requiring ownership, planning, or investment
- Green: adequate for current stage, with normal monitoring

## Expected Outcome

A concise technology posture assessment that identifies current state, material risks, ownership gaps, modernization priorities, and executive decisions required.
