# Technology Roadmap Template

## Purpose

Create a technology roadmap that ties technical initiatives to business objectives, owners, risks, cost, dependencies, and measurable outcomes.

## When to Use It

- During annual or quarterly planning
- After a technology assessment or diligence process
- When technical debt, platform modernization, or security work needs executive prioritization
- When the board or CEO needs visibility into technology investment decisions

## Key Questions

- What business objective does each initiative support?
- Who owns the outcome?
- What risk is reduced or opportunity enabled?
- What cost, timeline, and dependency assumptions matter?
- How will success be measured?

## Template or Framework

| Initiative | Business Objective | Owner | Priority | Risk | Cost | Timeline | Dependencies | Success Metric |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  | Revenue growth, retention, margin, compliance, resilience, speed, customer trust | Named executive or accountable leader | High / Medium / Low | Risk if delayed or not completed | Team time, vendor spend, cloud spend, opportunity cost | Target quarter or milestone | Teams, vendors, architecture, data, compliance | Observable business or operating metric |

### Roadmap Categories

- Platform modernization
- Security and compliance
- AI strategy and governance
- Data infrastructure
- Developer productivity
- Reliability and observability
- Cloud and Kubernetes operations
- Technical debt reduction
- Integration or post-close work

### Prioritization Criteria

Rank initiatives by:

1. Business impact
2. Risk reduction
3. Customer impact
4. Regulatory or contractual need
5. Cost and complexity
6. Dependency urgency
7. Reversibility

## Expected Outcome

A roadmap that makes technology investment decisions explicit, comparable, and tied to business outcomes rather than isolated engineering preferences.
