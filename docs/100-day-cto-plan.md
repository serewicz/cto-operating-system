# 100-Day CTO Plan

## Purpose

Provide a practical first 100-day operating plan for a CTO or technology executive entering a new company, transformation, investment cycle, or turnaround.

## When to Use It

- Joining a company as CTO or fractional CTO
- Preparing a technology organization for scale, diligence, or post-close integration
- Resetting technology leadership after delivery, security, or platform issues
- Creating a shared executive view of technology priorities

## Key Questions

- What does the business need technology to enable in the next 12-24 months?
- Which technology risks could materially affect revenue, customer trust, valuation, or operations?
- Who owns architecture, security, delivery, AI, infrastructure, and technical debt?
- Where are decisions blocked by unclear tradeoffs or lack of executive alignment?
- What should the board or CEO expect to see after 30, 60, and 100 days?

## Template or Framework

### Days 1-30: Understand

Objective: establish current state, trust, facts, and risk visibility.

| Area | Actions | Outputs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| People | Meet engineering, product, security, data, operations, finance, and executive stakeholders. Identify formal and informal technical leaders. | Stakeholder map, leadership observations, key-person risk notes |
| Architecture | Review system diagrams, runtime topology, integrations, critical services, data flows, and failure modes. | Current-state architecture summary |
| Delivery | Review release process, deployment frequency, incident history, roadmap commitments, and planning cadence. | Delivery predictability assessment |
| Security | Review identity, privileged access, vulnerability management, secrets, backups, logging, compliance needs, and incident process. | Security risk summary |
| AI | Inventory AI use cases, data sensitivity, model usage, vendor exposure, and human review practices. | AI use case and governance inventory |
| Infrastructure | Review cloud accounts, Kubernetes clusters, environments, observability, cost drivers, and operational ownership. | Infrastructure posture summary |
| Financials | Review technology spend, major vendors, cloud usage, tooling overlap, and contract commitments. | Technology cost baseline |
| Executive Communication | Establish CEO, board, and operating partner expectations for reporting cadence. | CTO communication plan |

### Days 31-60: Align

Objective: convert facts into priorities, ownership, and executive tradeoffs.

| Area | Actions | Outputs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| People | Clarify leadership gaps, role ambiguity, hiring needs, and succession risk. | Organization risk and capability map |
| Architecture | Identify modernization priorities, resilience gaps, integration risk, and architectural decision backlog. | Architecture priorities and ADR queue |
| Delivery | Align roadmap commitments with capacity, technical debt, and business priorities. | Delivery operating model |
| Security | Prioritize remediations by business impact and urgency. | Security remediation plan |
| AI | Define acceptable use, model selection principles, data handling rules, and review requirements. | AI governance draft |
| Infrastructure | Identify cost, reliability, scaling, and platform ownership improvements. | Platform improvement roadmap |
| Financials | Tie spend decisions to business value, risk reduction, and growth priorities. | Technology investment view |
| Executive Communication | Present top risks, tradeoffs, and recommended priorities to leadership. | Executive technology assessment |

### Days 61-100: Execute

Objective: begin measurable execution and establish durable operating cadence.

| Area | Actions | Outputs |
| --- | --- | --- |
| People | Close role ownership gaps, set leadership cadence, and launch hiring or vendor actions. | Team operating plan |
| Architecture | Start highest-value modernization or stabilization work. | Decision records and execution milestones |
| Delivery | Implement delivery metrics, roadmap governance, and dependency management. | Delivery review cadence |
| Security | Execute priority controls such as MFA, backup validation, access review, logging, or vulnerability triage. | Security progress report |
| AI | Approve or reject initial AI use cases based on governance criteria. | AI initiative register |
| Infrastructure | Launch reliability, Kubernetes, observability, FinOps, or platform improvements. | Infrastructure execution plan |
| Financials | Establish spend review and vendor decision process. | Technology budget and vendor review cadence |
| Executive Communication | Move from assessment to monthly operating review. | CTO monthly review and board-ready update |

## Expected Outcome

By day 100, leadership should have a clear view of technology posture, material risks, ownership, near-term priorities, and the operating cadence required to connect technology execution with business outcomes.
